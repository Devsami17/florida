SECTION 2 : Broker Services

Engagement: The Principal retains the Broker, as an independent contractor, to serve as the Broker of Record. The Broker accepts this role and agrees to perform services in the Principal's best interest.

Other Interests: The Broker maintains other full-time interests. The Principal has no claim on these other interests.

SECTION 4 : Term Of Agreement

Effective Date: When the Broker receives the DBPR License, and payment is received.

Termination: Either party may terminate this agreement with 60 days' written notice. Upon termination, the Principal is responsible for fees related to removing the Broker of Record from the Company charter with the Florida Secretary of State.

Indemnification: The Principal agrees to indemnify and hold the Broker harmless from any liability, including costs and expenses, arising from any misrepresentation, errors in contract information, or related legal actions. All legal costs associated with such actions will be borne by the Principal. Any legal action must be filed exclusively in Orange County, Florida.

Modification: This agreement supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.

Enforceability and Severability: If any term of this Agreement is deemed void or unenforceable, that term will be severed, and the remaining terms will remain in effect. Overbroad terms will be amended to make them enforceable.

Governing Law: This Agreement will be governed by the laws of the State of Florida.

Notices: Notices must be in writing and delivered either personally or via prepaid, certified mail. Notices are effective upon service or mailing. Parties may change their notice addresses by notifying the other party.

Successors and Assigns: This agreement binds the Principal and its successors and assigns. Any entity merging with or acquiring the Principal must assume the Principal's obligations under this Agreement, and the Broker must receive a copy of the assumption agreement.
